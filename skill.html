During my Ph.D., I developed statistical models to analyze the data obtained from financial entities, ranging from year 1979-2014. The title of my thesis was the Relationship of Macroeconomics variables on the financial sector. I developed Econometric models, which predicted the behavior of the Economy through macroeconomic variables. For instance, In layman's terms, we all know that oil prices affect a state economy quite significantly. Sharp rise and fall in oil prices put major stress over the financial entities of the state. Through my designed Econometric models, the financial entities will have an improved understanding of future events. In my thesis, I identified major variables, and by leveraging the advantage of probability theory and state-of-the-art Econometric solvers such as e-Views and STATA, I developed successful models that predicted the relationship of variables with more than 90% accuracy.
